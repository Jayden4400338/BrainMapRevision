Key Rules And Formulas

  • (x + a)(x + b) = x^2 + (a + b)x + ab
  • (x + p)(x + q)=0 gives x=-p or x=-q
  • x = [-b +/- sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)] / (2a)
  • Discriminant D = b^2 - 4ac
  • D>0 two roots, D=0 one, D<0 none real

Worked Examples

Expand (x+3)(x-2)

  1. Multiply terms and combine middle terms

Answer: x^2 + x - 6

Factorise x^2 + 7x + 12

  1. Find numbers that add to 7 and multiply to 12

Answer: (x + 3)(x + 4)

Solve x^2 - 9 = 0

  1. Factorise difference of squares then solve

Answer: x = 3 or -3

Q: Factorise x^2 + 8x + 15

x = 5 or -5

Q: Solve (x - 4)(x + 2)=0

x = 4 or -2

Q: For x^2 + 2x + 1 discriminant

Q: Expand (2x + 1)(x - 3)

Common Mistakes

  • Not setting equation equal to zero
  • Wrong use of plus/minus in formula
  • Ignoring discriminant meaning
