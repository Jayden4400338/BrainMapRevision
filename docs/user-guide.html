1. Getting Started

Welcome to BrainMapRevision! This platform is designed to help you master every subject through interactive revision tools, past paper practice, and personalized study boards.

What is BrainMapRevision?

BrainMapRevision is a free, open-source revision platform for students from Year 1 to Year 11. Whether you're preparing for SATs or GCSEs, we provide:

  • Authentic past paper questions from major UK exam boards
  • Comprehensive revision guides for key topics
  • Interactive quizzes and flashcards
  • Custom revision board creator
  • Progress tracking and analytics
  • Gamified learning with XP and rewards

System Requirements

BrainMapRevision works on:

  • Modern web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
  • Desktop, tablet, and mobile devices
  • No downloads or installations required

2. Creating an Account

Sign Up Process

  1. Click "Get Started Free" on the homepage
  2. Choose your role: Student or Teacher
  3. Enter your email address and create a password
  4. Verify your email (check your inbox)
  5. Complete your profile with year group and subjects

Student vs Teacher Accounts

Student Accounts:

  • Access all study materials and resources
  • Create personal revision boards
  • Track your own progress
  • Earn XP and Brain Coins
  • Join classrooms created by teachers

Teacher Accounts:

  • All student features, plus:
  • Create and manage classrooms
  • Assign homework and track student progress
  • View class analytics
  • Share resources with students

4. Creating Revision Boards

What are Revision Boards?

Revision boards are customizable study spaces where you can organize notes, flashcards, and practice questions by topic or subject.

Creating a Board

  1. Navigate to "Revision Boards" in the menu
  2. Click "Create New Board"
  3. Name your board (e.g., "Macbeth Revision")
  4. Select subject and topics
  5. Add content: notes, flashcards, or questions
  6. Customize with colors and tags
  7. Save your board

Adding Content to Boards

  • Text Notes: Type or paste revision notes
  • Flashcards: Create question/answer cards
  • Past Paper Questions: Add from the question bank
  • Images: Upload diagrams or screenshots
  • Links: Add external resources

Organizing Boards

  • Group boards by subject or exam
  • Use tags for easy searching
  • Share boards with classmates (optional)
  • Export boards as PDFs

5. Using Past Papers

Accessing Past Paper Questions

  1. Go to "Past Papers" in the navigation
  2. Filter by subject, exam board, and year
  3. Browse available questions
  4. Click on a question to view details

Question Information

Each question includes:

  • Exam board, year, and paper number
  • Mark allocation
  • Official mark scheme
  • Examiner comments
  • Comprehensive revision guide
  • Related topic links

Practice Modes

Study Mode:

  • View questions with instant access to mark schemes
  • Read revision guides alongside questions
  • Take your time to understand concepts

Test Mode:

  • Timed practice under exam conditions
  • Mark schemes hidden until completion
  • Earn bonus XP for high scores

Revision Guides

Each past paper question has an accompanying guide that explains:

  • Key concepts being tested
  • Step-by-step approach to answering
  • Common mistakes to avoid
  • Exam technique tips
  • Related practice questions

6. Taking Quizzes

Starting a Quiz

  1. Navigate to "Quizzes"
  2. Select your subject and topic
  3. Choose difficulty level (Foundation/Higher)
  4. Set number of questions (5, 10, 20, or custom)
  5. Enable timer if desired
  6. Click "Start Quiz"

Question Types

  • Multiple choice
  • Short answer
  • Fill in the blank
  • True/False
  • Extended response

Using Hints

Stuck on a question? Use hint tokens to get help:

  • Remove 2 wrong answers (1 hint) - Multiple choice only
  • Show topic/keyword (1 hint)
  • Reveal first letter (1 hint) - Short answer
  • Show related formula (2 hints) - Math/Science
  • Display worked example (3 hints)
  • Full answer explanation (5 hints)

Earn free hints through daily logins, perfect scores, and level-ups. Or purchase hint packs in the shop with Brain Coins.

Scoring and XP

  • Complete quiz: +20 XP, +10 Brain Coins
  • Perfect score bonus: +50 XP, +25 Brain Coins
  • Using hints reduces XP earned slightly
  • Detailed feedback on incorrect answers

7. Using Flashcards

Accessing Flashcards

  • Browse pre-made flashcard decks by subject
  • Create your own custom decks
  • Add flashcards to revision boards

Creating Flashcards

  1. Go to "Flashcards" → "Create Deck"
  2. Name your deck (e.g., "Photosynthesis Key Terms")
  3. Add cards with question on front, answer on back
  4. Include images if helpful
  5. Tag with relevant topics

Study Sessions

BrainMapRevision uses spaced repetition to optimize learning:

  • Cards you struggle with appear more frequently
  • Mastered cards appear less often
  • Track mastery level for each card
  • Earn XP for completing flashcard sessions

Flashcard Tips

  • Study in short sessions (10-15 minutes)
  • Review daily for best retention
  • Say answers out loud before flipping
  • Mark cards honest ("Easy", "Medium", "Hard")

8. XP & Leveling System

How XP Works

Experience Points (XP) represent your learning progress. Earn XP by:

Study Activities:

  • Complete a quiz: +20 XP
  • Perfect score bonus: +50 XP
  • Answer past paper question: +15 XP
  • Review flashcards (10 cards): +10 XP
  • Create revision board: +25 XP
  • 30-minute study session: +30 XP
  • Daily login: +5 XP
  • 7-day streak: +100 XP bonus

Community Contributions:

  • Submit past paper question: +25 XP
  • Write revision guide: +50 XP
  • Helpful forum answer (per upvote): +10 XP
  • Report error: +5 XP

Achievements:

  • First quiz completed: +100 XP
  • 100 questions answered: +250 XP
  • Subject mastery (90%+): +500 XP
  • Help 10 students: +200 XP

Leveling Up

Level requirements:

  • Level 1: 0 XP (Start)
  • Level 2: 100 XP
  • Level 3: 250 XP
  • Level 5: 1,000 XP
  • Level 10: 5,000 XP
  • Level 25: 25,000 XP
  • Level 50: 100,000 XP
  • Level 100: 500,000 XP

Level Perks

  • Level 5: Unlock custom theme creator
  • Level 10: Daily coin bonus +10
  • Level 15: Unlock exclusive themes
  • Level 25: Access to 2x XP events
  • Level 50: Premium badge
  • Level 100: Legendary badge + custom profile URL

11. Classroom Features (Teachers)

Creating a Classroom

  1. Navigate to "Classroom" → "Create New"
  2. Name your classroom (e.g., "Year 10 History")
  3. Select subject and year group
  4. Generate invite code or link
  5. Share code with students

Managing Students

  • View student roster
  • Track individual progress
  • See completion rates
  • Remove or add students

Creating Assignments

  1. Select "Assignments" in classroom dashboard
  2. Click "Create New Assignment"
  3. Choose content: quiz, past papers, or board
  4. Set due date
  5. Assign to entire class or individuals

Class Analytics

  • View class average scores
  • Identify struggling students
  • Track assignment completion
  • Generate progress reports

For Students: Joining a Classroom

  1. Receive invite code from teacher
  2. Navigate to "Classroom" → "Join"
  3. Enter invite code
  4. Confirm joining
  5. Access classroom resources and assignments

12. Study Tips & Best Practices

Effective Revision Strategies

  • Start Early: Begin revision weeks before exams, not days
  • Study in Chunks: 25-30 minute sessions with 5-minute breaks
  • Mix It Up: Alternate between subjects and topics
  • Practice Regularly: Do a little every day rather than cramming
  • Test Yourself: Quizzes are more effective than just reading

Using BrainMapRevision Effectively

  • Complete daily challenges to maintain your study streak
  • Focus on topics where you score lowest in quizzes
  • Review flashcards multiple times for better retention
  • Practice past papers under timed conditions
  • Read revision guides even for questions you answer correctly

Maximizing XP and Coins

  • Log in daily for streak bonuses
  • Aim for perfect quiz scores
  • Complete weekly challenges
  • Contribute quality content to earn community rewards
  • Use 2x XP boosts during intensive study sessions

Preparing for Exams

  • 6 weeks before: Create comprehensive revision boards
  • 4 weeks before: Focus on weak topics, use quizzes
  • 2 weeks before: Practice past papers under exam conditions
  • 1 week before: Review flashcards and revision guides
  • Night before: Light review, get good sleep

Getting Help

  • Use the community forum to ask questions
  • Check revision guides for detailed explanations
  • Request new topics if content is missing
  • Report errors to help improve the platform
  • Collaborate with classmates in shared boards
